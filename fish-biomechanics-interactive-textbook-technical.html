A reduced theory of fish swimming

We are building one usable model, not a catalogue of every fish detail. The path is simple: draw a body wave, ask how water pushes back, pay the drag cost, then add wake timing, muscle filtering, fins, and outside vortices. If a term stays in the model, it must change something you can see in the diagram.

The model we keep

The reduced model keeps only effects you can see and tune: body-wave shape, added-mass thrust, drag, Strouhal wake quality, the body’s amplitude filter, fin assist, and work from environmental wakes. It is small enough to animate, but each term still points to a physical mechanism in the papers.
